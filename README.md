# The Luxury Correction: Who Held, Who Broke, and Why

**A data-driven analysis of the 2024–2025 luxury market reset across LVMH, Hermès, Kering, Richemont, and Burberry.**

🔗 **[View Live Dashboard](https://kavitaadha.github.io/luxury-market-analysis)**

---

## Overview

Between 2021 and 2023, the global luxury market rode a post-COVID supercycle. By 2024, that cycle ended — Chinese consumer spending dropped ~20%, aspirational buyers exited, and revenue declined across most major groups.

But the correction wasn't uniform.

**Hermès grew +15%. Kering fell -12%.** The gap between them is not luck — it's the measurable distance between authentic brand equity and inflated positioning.

This dashboard visualises the data behind that divergence.

## Key Findings

- **Brand equity leads revenue by 12–18 months** — Kering's signal was deteriorating long before earnings reflected it
- **Hermès maintained a 42% operating margin** through the correction — the highest in the industry
- **Gucci's wholesale fell 53% in Q4 2024** — a structural collapse, not a temporary dip
- **Prada posted 20 consecutive quarters of growth** into 2025 while peers declined
- **The correction hit aspirational buyers hardest** — brands with ultra-HNWI depth were insulated
